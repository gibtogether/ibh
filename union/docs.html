§1 · Overview Local 4663

Hold $HOOD. Collect union dividends in stocks — not more $HOOD.

Local 4663 runs a fee → buy → airdrop stack on Robinhood Chain (chain id 4663). You hold $HOOD; when the hall runs, you receive tokenized stock tokens (BB, AMC, NOK, GME) — not additional $HOOD.

No staking, no claim ritual. Eligible card-carrying members are paid pro-rata on the hall’s distribution cadence.

§2 · The fee story Rules of the hall

One clean story

Everything below is the official description of how dues fund dividends. Pool LP fees may still apply on swaps; they are separate from the distribution fee.

1 · Who pays

  • Buys and sells. A 3.5% distribution fee is taken on both buys and sells of $HOOD through the Uniswap V4 hook (IndexFeeHook). The fee is collected in native ETH.
  • Pool LP fees are separate. Ordinary Uniswap pool fees may still apply on top of the distribution fee.

2 · Where it goes

  • Hook → treasury. The distribution fee is routed to StockTreasury (dues collected).
  • Treasury → stock buy. The treasury uses those dues to buy the dividend basket of stock tokens (BB, AMC, NOK, GME).
  • Distributor → members. Stock tokens move to StockDistributor, which pays eligible members pro-rata on each run.

dues path: trade fee → treasury → stock buy → distributor → member wallets

3 · Who is eligible

  • No minimum holdings. Any wallet with a positive $HOOD balance at snapshot / distribute time is eligible for that run (BANG-style). Dust balances still count; share is pro-rata.
  • Share: each eligible member’s cut is proportional to their $HOOD balance relative to the sum of all eligible balances.
  • Optional floor later: the protocol can raise a minimum later via setMinShareBalance if needed — default today is any (zero floor).

4 · Cadence

  • Next run: distributions run on a fixed interval of about 30 minutes (configured as distributionInterval).
  • Union Books shows Next dividend run from that schedule so members can see when the books post next.
  • A run pays out stock tokens already acquired; if the treasury has not yet bought for a cycle, the ledger will reflect that honestly when live chain reads are wired.
§3 · Dividend cycle Keeper flow

From trade to member receipt

  1. Dues collected A buy or sell of $HOOD hits the V4 pool. The hook routes the 3.5% ETH fee to the treasury.
  2. Treasury buys stock tokens Keeper calls treasury / distributor buy paths (V4 and/or Rialto with USDG). Stock tokens move to the distributor.
  3. Interval elapsed After the distribution interval (~30 minutes), a new cycle may start.
  4. Snapshot holders Holder list is snapshotted from the reflection token (any positive $HOOD, not excluded).
  5. Distribute batch startCycle → distributeBatch until every eligible member has been paid pro-rata for that run.
  6. Posted on the ledger Transfers appear on the explorer. Union Books and Distributions list each run; members can pull a membership dividend receipt for their share.
§4 · Union Books The product

The books are the product

Union Books is Local 4663’s live statement of accounts — not a trading terminal. When chain wiring is live, figures read from public contracts and explorers: no admin spreadsheet, no trust-me totals.

  • Total paid — historical value of stock tokens distributed
  • Dues collected — fees received by the treasury
  • Card-carrying members — roll of holders with any positive $HOOD
  • Next dividend run — countdown / scheduled window

Supporting metrics (price, treasury balance, fees, next distribution) and the Distributions ledger sit under the same idea: official records of the local.

The Union Hall is the community room — our Telegram — not a tab in this app. Books are the records; the Hall is where members gather.

§5 · Membership receipts Personal record

Membership dividend receipt

After clock-in, a member can view a membership dividend receipt for a run — styled like a hall slip, not a degen PnL card.

  • Card number and member record
  • Run time / period
  • Stock tokens received (per ticker) and estimated USD mark
  • Link to the on-chain transaction

Receipts live on Portfolio (latest / featured slip) and in personal history under Distributions → Your activity. They are proof of what the hall paid you — stocks, not more $HOOD.

§7 · Data sources What powers the UI

Where numbers come from

  • ReflectionToken $HOOD balances, holder list (any positive balance).
  • IndexFeeHook · StockTreasury Fees collected, treasury ETH, stock purchase events.
  • StockDistributor Cycle timing, batch distribution events, member receipts.
  • Uniswap V4 PoolManager $HOOD / ETH quote for price metrics and swap UI.
  • Stock token pools Per-asset prices on the dividend board.
  • Blockscout (Robinhood Chain) Transaction links for every ledger row.
  • App mock layer (pre-launch) Until RPC wiring lands, js/mock-data.js supplies demo figures so Union Books can be reviewed end-to-end.
§8 · App map Tabs + community

What each tab is for

  • Books — Union Books home: statement of accounts, latest run, membership office, board, recent runs.
  • Swap — Buy $HOOD. Closed until launch; live desk after pool + config are set.
  • Portfolio — Your balance, share, stocks earned, membership dividend receipt.
  • Distributions — Full history: dividend runs, treasury buys, your activity. Rows → txs.
  • Docs — This brief (fee story, books, receipts).
  • Union Hall — Telegram community (not an app tab). Link in the footer when configured.

Clock in verifies membership for personal panels. Wallet connect replaces the local mock before go-live.
